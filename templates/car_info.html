Understanding Vehicle Emissions Standards

Emissions standards vary by country and region. Learn about the major standards like Euro 6, EPA Tier 3, and CARB, and what they mean for vehicle owners.

Published: June 2023

Emissions standards are regulations that set specific limits on the amount of pollutants that can be released into the atmosphere. They are designed to reduce air pollution and its associated health and environmental impacts.

The major emissions standards include:

  • Euro Standards (Europe): Currently at Euro 6 for passenger cars, which sets strict limits on nitrogen oxides (NOx), carbon monoxide (CO), hydrocarbons (THC and NMHC) and particulate matter (PM).
  • EPA Tier 3 (United States): These standards focus on reducing both tailpipe and evaporative emissions from passenger cars, light-duty trucks, medium-duty passenger vehicles, and some heavy-duty vehicles.
  • CARB (California Air Resources Board): Often sets stricter standards than federal regulations, with many states choosing to follow CARB standards.

For vehicle owners, these regulations typically mean newer vehicles are cleaner but may have more complex emissions control systems that require proper maintenance.

The Evolution of Electric Vehicles

From early experiments to modern Tesla models, learn about how electric vehicles have evolved and what the future holds for this rapidly growing segment.

Published: July 2023

Electric vehicles (EVs) have a surprisingly long history, dating back to the 1830s when the first crude electric carriage was invented. By the early 1900s, electric cars were actually more popular than gasoline vehicles in many areas.

The modern EV revolution began in the late 1990s with vehicles like the GM EV1 and Toyota RAV4 EV, but truly accelerated with the introduction of the Tesla Roadster in 2008, which proved electric vehicles could be both high-performance and desirable.

Today's EVs feature:

  • Lithium-ion battery packs with increasingly longer ranges
  • Fast-charging capabilities that reduce charging times significantly
  • Advanced motor technology with improved efficiency
  • Sophisticated battery management systems for optimal performance and safety

The future of EVs looks promising with upcoming solid-state batteries expected to further increase range while reducing weight and charging time.
